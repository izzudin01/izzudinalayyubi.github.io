Steam → Electric Cartridge Heater Retrofit (CAPEX Study)

Industrial heating retrofit feasibility study for a rework press: convert centralized steam heating to localized electric cartridge heaters.

Objective

  • Improve mold temperature stability (target: 164 ± 2°C).
  • Reduce operational complexity and improve maintainability.
  • Provide management-ready CAPEX justification (ROI/NPV/IRR).

Context

  • Existing system uses boiler/steam distribution and valves.
  • Proposed solution: localized electric cartridge heaters per mold + better control.
  • Stakeholders: engineering, production, maintenance, management.

My Role & Deliverables

  • Defined electrical integration concept: power distribution, protective devices, grounding approach, and safe wiring philosophy.
  • Designed control concept: on-off → PI/PID time-proportional control strategy (implementation-ready concept level).
  • Built a techno-economic model to support CAPEX decision-making (ROI / NPV / IRR).
  • Maintained structured engineering documentation (risk register, assumptions, and traceable decision inputs).

Approach

  • Identified failure modes and safety requirements (over-temp, interlocks, protection coordination).
  • Translated process needs into control blocks and boundary conditions (time-proportional heating).
  • Structured model assumptions to enable sensitivity checks (energy price / duty cycle / utilization).

Tools & Methods

AutoCAD Electrical COMSOL (thermal) Excel (ROI/NPV/IRR) Risk Register Control Concept

Impact (Business & Operations)

  • Delivered a structured CAPEX justification package for internal review and decision making.
  • Improved clarity of design intent through documented safety philosophy and control strategy.
  • Reduced ambiguity for cross-functional execution (engineering ↔ maintenance ↔ production).
