Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) Design

Analog/RF learning project: common-source amplifier with resistive feedback, focused on design tradeoffs and structured verification.

Objective

  • Design an LNA topology with stable biasing and predictable gain.
  • Understand tradeoffs: gain vs noise vs linearity vs stability.
  • Document design decisions and key equations/assumptions.

What It Covers

  • Common-source stage with resistive feedback.
  • Biasing strategy and small-signal parameters.
  • Basic performance checks (gain, bandwidth, stability concept).

Approach

  • Defined target specs (gain/bandwidth constraints) and selected topology.
  • Calculated bias points and small-signal behavior (gm, ro, feedback effect).
  • Iterated component values to balance gain, input matching tendency, and stability margin.
  • Recorded assumptions and limitations (learning-focused scope).

Tools & Tech

Analog Design Small-Signal Analysis Feedback Simulation (if used) Documentation

UT MT Angle

  • Shows structured engineering thinking and tradeoff management.
  • Demonstrates discipline in assumptions and documentation.
  • Signals ability to learn complex technical topics systematically.
