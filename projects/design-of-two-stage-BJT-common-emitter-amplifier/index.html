Two-Stage BJT Common-Emitter Amplifier

Electronics learning project: biasing and gain design for a two-stage BJT common-emitter amplifier with practical tradeoffs.

Objective

  • Design stable biasing for repeatable amplifier operation.
  • Achieve target gain using a two-stage approach.
  • Understand distortion/headroom constraints and coupling choices.

Key Concepts

  • Operating point design (Q-point).
  • Small-signal gain and loading between stages.
  • Stability via emitter degeneration and bias network choices.

Approach

  • Selected bias network to stabilize Q-point against β variation.
  • Estimated gain per stage and considered inter-stage loading.
  • Used degeneration/feedback concepts to trade gain for stability/linearity.
  • Documented calculations and results for clarity and repeatability.
