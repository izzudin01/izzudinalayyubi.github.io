Two-Stage CMOS Operational Amplifier Design

Analog IC learning project: two-stage op-amp design with focus on gain, biasing, and stability/compensation concepts.

Objective

  • Build a two-stage op-amp architecture and understand each block’s role.
  • Analyze key metrics: DC gain, bandwidth, stability (phase margin concept).
  • Practice design iteration and verification workflow.

Key Concepts

  • Biasing and operating point consistency.
  • Compensation (e.g., Miller capacitor concept) and stability tradeoffs.
  • Load effect and output swing limitations.

Approach

  • Defined block-level goals (input stage, gain stage, output behavior).
  • Estimated small-signal gain per stage; checked headroom constraints.
  • Applied compensation concept to improve stability.
  • Documented assumptions and results for iterative improvement.
